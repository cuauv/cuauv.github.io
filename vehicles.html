Our 2018 Competition Subs

Two Brothers Castor & Pollux

CUAUV pioneered the AUVSI competition in 2016 by building not one but two subs, Thor and Loki. We continue this proud tradition with our brand new vehicles Castor and Pollux. Castor and Pollux were demigods and the patron saints of sailors. With CUAUV, Castor and Pollux return to the sea at AUVSI summer 2018.

Our Technologies

Mechanical

The mechanical team uses Solidworks to design the custom enclosures, racks, and structures on the vehicles. Throughout the design process, rigorous finite element analysis is run on each assembly to ensure every part will perform successfully under high loads. Every component is manufactured in house with mills, lathes, and CNC machines to a tolerance of 0.005 inches. Mechanical members then integrate and test all assemblies up to fifty feet underwater. Recent developments on the mechanical team have included interchangeable battery pods between the subs, custom heatsinks for the PCBs, and a dual hydrophones system.

Electrical

The electrical subteams works to connect the software running on the main computer with the rest of the submarine, ensuring efciency with the vehicle’s power, sensor, and mechatronics systems. Team members design, layout, and populate custom printed circuit boards (PCBs), which all connect via an integrated backplane. New improvements have allowed the entire backbone of the submarines to be modular and nearly identical across both vehicles. This improves the functionality and reliability of both machines. Battery management boards allow for reliable monitoring and protection against power spikes. Additionally, the Power over Ethernet (PoE) boards allow for ease of power distribution without the need for a large amount of cables. Lastly, the redesigned hydrophones system includes an active pinger to enable the vehicles to communicate, and an FPGA for onboard signal processing.

Software

The software team continues to improve the numerous systems that make up the CUAUV software stack. A new autonomous mission planner is being developed to improve dynamic decision making during runs. Additionally, a novel approach is being introduced to our computer vision stack to take advantage of neural networks.
